Do certain ethnicities show higher levels of parental education?

Select a level of education to comapre against the five ethnicities in this study:

From selecting the various degree levels, it appears that Group C has the best educated parents in general (apart from Group D, who have more parents with a masters degree), and Group A and E have the lowest numbers of educated parents across the degree levels.

However, does this show the true picture?

Lets look at these as percentages of the total of each ethniity. Select a level of education on the legend to the right of the graph to compare across ethnicities.

We can see here that, through loking at the proportion of each ethnicity in relation to their parent's level of education that group A has the highest proportion in lower levels of education of education (with almost 70% either only having some high school, high school or some college), whereas over half of groups C and E have higher levels of education (i.e. associate degree, bachelor degree or masters degree). It is particularaly interesting to see Group E in this light, as although in the previous graph we thought that Group E were lower in relation to parental level of education, we see that this was skewed due to the low numbers in this group. However, when looking at the percentages within the group, they are among the most highly educated.
